# Rhythm of The Session
Your game leader will present opportunities to engage with the world. As you do, quests will reveal themselves that will include opportunities to connect, explore, fight, and solve. 
## Scenarios
Each game session is episodic and presents a scenario, multiple smaller scenarios (think: Tales of Ba Sing Se), or a single act of a greater scenario. That scenario has a dramatic question that identifies the defining condition as to when the scenario reaches its conclusion, regardless of how. 

Once the answer to a dramatic question is clear and unavoidable, the scenario is brought to a close. The GM will narrate through the final moments and transition to the next stage. Answering the dramatic question favorably is the primary objective of the player characters.
## Session Loop
Most of the game follows a simple pattern:

1. The GM sets the stage & presents a scenario, then clearly invites players to act.
2. The players ask questions, investigate, explore, and declare actions. 
3. The GM adjudicates actions. When appropriate the dice are rolled. The roll total helps determine the results and the game leader translates those into the narrative.
4. The GM describes how things change and transitions the focus to the next moment of opportunity.
## Ending The Session
As the session is brought to a close, the table answers these questions together:

1. What happened?
2. What do we want to remember? What did we learn? Make note of these things.
3. What are we doing next in the game?
### Assigning XP
Next, the GM assigns equal XP to each party member. They gain 1 XP when the scenario is brought to a close. This is the completion of the quest, for better or for worse. Answering it victoriously grants 1 additional XP. This is completing it well. Further XP can be gained under the following conditions based on your GM’s feeling:

- If you suffered no major losses, gain 1 XP
- If you found optional content, gain 1 XP
- If you completed optional scenario objectives, gain 1 XP
- If you exemplified teamwork, gain 1 XP
### Prepare For Next Session
Before the players each go their separate ways, the table spends no more than 5 minutes loosely scheduling in the most likely dates for the next two gaming sessions and grants the GM permission to follow-up with each member of the table in advance of those dates.

The table may also briefly discuss other plans or back-up plans as necessary, including sub-plots, other games for game night, budget-friendly local real-life activities, serving, uplifting, or celebrating someone at the table, or rescheduling an upcoming session.

Finally, the GM takes simple notes on the scenario:

Bullet point events from the session. What happened and when? Or at least, what order?
Bullet point NPCs from the session. Who did the players meet (what are they known as, and what “character background” phrase best describes them) and where? 
What can you bring full circle later (sooner or later) by bringing rewards and consequences back around to the players directly due to this session?

These notes, as well as clearly knowing what the player characters will be doing next are used by the GM later for planning the next session.
