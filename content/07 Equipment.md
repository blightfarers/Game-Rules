## Tools, Gear, Facilities
Usually tools offer one or more of these types of benefits:
Guarantee success at the cost of time, money, or other resources
Count as an Asset for certain rolls
Make certain acts possible
Improvised tools might do so with disadvantage
Low quality ones might have a small Hindrance attached
Increase the speed of an action
Allow the benefit of specific traits or abilities

## Armor
Armor offers Protection, which reduces damage received on a 1:1 ratio before counting it against your WT.

When armor is donned in a rush, taking only one turn, it includes only the most vital or essential parts of the armor set (this might be a helm, gloves, & breastplate for example). Reduce its normal Protection rating by 2. Donning a full-body armor suit takes several minutes. 

Against special damage types such as Frost, Fire, Shock, or Acid the Protection rating of armor is halved unless the armor is Resistant to that damage type.

By default, all armor causes the wearer to suffer Disadvantage on checks to swim or stay afloat.

Armor may have additional drawbacks including:

- <u></u>Heavy: endurance checks have Disadvantage, and checks to swim or stay afloat automatically fail.
- <u></u>Loud: checks to move silently have Disadvantage.
- <u></u>Rigid: checks where acrobatics and flexible movement are a focus have Disadvantage.
- <u></u>Flashy: this armor draws attention; you take Disadvantage to hide or if you are not hiding, others take Advantage to spot you - whichever is most appropriate in the circumstances.

Armor may also have compensatory or beneficial traits, including:

- <u></u>Cuttable: this armor is made with straps or cords that can be cut in an emergency. Others might be able to cut your straps as well, but they suffer Disadvantage to do so if you are mobile and resisting.
- <u></u>Swimmable: this armor does not cause Disadvantage on swimming checks after all, even if the armor is Heavy.
- <u></u>Breathable: this armor does not provide Disadvantage on endurance checks after all, even if the armor is Heavy.
- <u></u>Spiked: this armor functions as a 2d6 and automatically deals its damage each round you are grappling or grappled.
- <u></u>Insulating: this armor grants you Advantage to withstand freezing temperatures.
- <u></u>Camouflaged: this armor blends in granting Advantage to hide.
- <u></u>Resistant: Against listed special attack types the armor provides its normal Protection rating instead of half. Against common attack types the armor provides + 2 Protection.
- <u></u>Glancing: this armor is a +1 Asset to all defense checks.

Armor may also provoke modified behavior from certain creatures or groups:

- <u></u>Pacifies (X): alters behavior of listed creatures, such that they react neutrally towards you until provoked.
- <u></u>Angers (X): alters behavior of listed creatures, if reasonable they will aim their aggression at you before others, or plan to do so when they can get a tempting opportunity.
- <u></u>Intimidates (X): provokes altered behavior from listed creatures, causing them to be uncomfortable and avoidant when in your presence and to avoid hunting you when other options may be present.

Less common armor traits are listed as special effects and described separately.

### Shields
Shields are a miracle for combatants. It is useful as an improvised weapon. It offers protection simply by being held, it can provide cover, or include a boss specifically for deflecting even powerful melee attacks. Even lighter and smaller shields are incredibly useful.

Shields are a 1 point Asset for rolls that involve protecting yourself or another from physical harm. If the shield is Reinforced, this is a 2 point Asset instead. Shields may have additional traits:

- <u>*Bossed*</u> shields have an additional 2 point Asset against melee attacks.
- <u>*Wide*</u> shields have an additional 2 point Asset against ranged or aoe attacks.
- <u>*Tall*</u> shields grant cover against ranged attacks, granting Disadvantage to ranged & aoe attackers.
- <u>*Throwing*</u> shields can be used without penalty as throwing weapons.
- <u>*Strapped*</u> shields can be worn instead of held. Like with other armor, these straps can be cut.
- <u>*Spiked*</u> shields deal 2d6 when used as a weapon.

## Weapons & Unarmed Attacks
Weapons enable more effective lethal actions. They protect you by giving you the ability to threaten others who would do you and your party harm. As per the rules in the Combat section they may be used to perform a variety of offensive maneuvers, and also utility maneuvers. 

Ranged attacks made while under melee threat suffer Disadvantage.

Weapons are defined by traits as well as damage:

- <u>*Unarmed*</u>: a placeholder tag for martial arts to be defined later
- <u>*Long*</u>: these weapons suffer Disadvantage in grapples, intimately close ranges, or in tight and cluttered areas. When your adversary is in range of your weapon but you are not in range of theirs, take Advantage.
- <u>*Shatter*</u>: these attacks deals 2 extra damage and gains Advantage on damage rolls vs objects
- <u>*Cleave*</u>: on Triumph Ranks-Up all damage die once and gains Advantage on damage roll
- <u>*Precise*</u>: on Triumph may choose to ignore armor instead of a normal Triumph benefit
- <u>*Efficient*</u>: these weapons are a 1-point Asset for to-hit and defending
- <u>*2-Handed*</u>: these weapons require both hands to use effectively. Unwieldy otherwise.
- <u>*Unwieldy*</u>: these weapons suffer Disadvantage on to-hit.
- <u>*Heavy*</u>: if your Power is less than 4, take a 1 point Hindrance when using this weapon for attack or defense.
- <u>*Flailing*</u>: attacks with these weapons ignore the effects of shields
- <u>*Throw*</u>: can be used for Throwing attacks without Disadvantage for improvised Throwing. If another range is listed in parenthesis then the throwing range is limited or extended compared to normal.
- <u>*Lucky*</u>: damage dice explode on their maximum result
- <u>*Loading*</u>: weapons cannot be used for a multi-attack at range
- <u>*Short Range*</u>: weapons can attack farther than throwing up to a moderate distance
- <u>*Far Range*</u>: weapons can attack as far as you can see in ideal conditions
- <u>*Bouncing*</u>: attacks commonly ricochet, requiring a reaction from someone nearby to not be hit.
- <u>*Tripping*</u>: weapons may optionally knock the target prone on a Triumph instead of a normal Triumph benefit.
