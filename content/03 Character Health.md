---
title: Character Health
---
No matter how skilled you are your health can effect how effective you can afford to be. In Blight Farers, the struggle against death is battle of tug of war - how well you pace yourself and coordinate for the well-being of your whole team matters exceedingly.
## Health Track
You have a Health Track which has 5 Health Levels. When something is impactful enough or has built up enough it leads to you losing one of the Levels in your Health Track. If you’ve only got two Health Levels or less remaining you are Battered (these last two Health Levels have a dividing line & boxes around them).

<u>_Battered_</u>: suffer Disadvantage on all actions that are not about defending, disengaging, suing for peace, or otherwise focused defensively on keeping you alive. If you would become Battered while already so, become Vulnerable instead until one of the effects causing you to be Battered ends.

You will typically lose a Health Level from the cascading effects of Ailing Conditions or from taking damage.

## Wound Threshold
When you successfully hit with an attack, you may deal damage by rolling your weapon damage and adding the appropriate Attribute - usually Power or Agility, or Potency for spells.

When you take damage, track it on your character sheet beside your Wound Threshold (WT). 

	Your WT equals 2 + Vitality + (½ Spirit).

Whenever accumulated damage equals or exceeds your WT, lose one Health Level then subtract WT from the damage total that your tracking. If sufficient damage remains you may have to repeat this process multiple times.

For example, if you have a WT of 3 and take 5 damage, you’ll lose a health level and tally the other 2 points of damage. If you previously had taken 1 damage, instead you’ll end up losing 2 Health Levels with no tally’s for damage remaining.
## Ailing Conditions
When it comes to your Ailing Conditions, you’ve got 3 to stay aware of - Tired, Hungry, & Distressed. These are binary states; either you are Hungry, or you're not. While you have an Ailing Condition, put a mark in its checkbox. When you get to remove that condition, erase that mark.

Additionally, if you would have to mark an Ailing Condition while you already have it marked, the effect Cascades, requiring you to mark one of the other two Ailing Conditions. You choose which. If you cannot because all of them are marked, you lose a Health Level instead.

None of the Ailing Conditions apply any penalties while you have them. They are a warning and a buffer - a signal that you are functionally nearing degraded health.

- You mark Tired if you didn’t get at least poor sleep (~ more than 4 hours) or you Fumble a check for Endurance. 
- You mark Hungry if you either didn’t hydrate or get at least 2 poor meals (rations) during the day.
- You mark Distressed when you fail a Check for morale such as due to violence, horror, despair, or deep disgust.
## Starred Conditions
Sometimes your GM will make note of special circumstances that help you flourish and ask you to put a star beside one of the Ailing Conditions. While you have an Ailing Condition starred, the next time you would mark it, remove the star instead.

Each starred condition grants a +1 Asset to your Daily Recovery, Sickness, & Death and to healing checks made by your caretakers.

- If you had plenty of sleep (~9+ hours) in comfortable conditions, or took a day off from adventure in a place of safety, you should star Tired.
- If you had a filling and hearty meal or ate well during feasts & festivities, you should star Hungry.
- If you are exceedingly prepared for the path ahead of you, are especially faithfully centered & determined, or uplifted by the true companionship of your fellows, you should star Distressed.

If you have an ailing condition marked but would get to star it, instead remove the mark. This is the primary way to clear marked ailing conditions.
## Daily Recovery
The world is striving to heal itself - and that includes you. Each dawn you make a Vitality Check for Daily Recovery with a difficulty of 13. In certain areas of the world the difficulty of this check may be altered or certain environments might be considered an Asset or Hindrance to this roll.

- On a Hit, restore one lost Health Level. 
- On a Triumph restore 2. 

If dawn occurs during an encounter, the GM will have you make this check either right before or right after.

[[Medical Aid]] and [[Health Track#Starred Conditions|starred conditions]] can help you recover more quickly.
## Medical Aid
A caretaker or physician may make checks, with time and necessary resources, to tend to the needs of the suffering. 

When a caretaker has basic medial supplies or can improvise them and gives medical aid to another character, the patient clears any leftover tallied damage that did not meet the Wound Threshold (and therefore, did not become a lost Health Level).

While a caretaker may not directly restore one of your lost Health Levels they can provide long term monitoring and care that improves your chances of recovery.

When a caretaker provides care specific to their patients necessities by explicitly treating the ailment, cleaning wounds, monitors them, and re-dresses wounds or re-applies treatment as needed over the course of hours or more they may make a Medicine Check.

- On a Hit, the patient gains a +2 Asset on their next Daily Recovery, Sickness, and Death Checks. 
- On a Triumph they gain Advantage in addition to the +2.

 The base difficulty for such Checks is at least 13. Special effects & circumstances may modify the difficulty of checks for healing like normal.

Tools and time are almost always required, although some resources may be improvised. Plus, filth and infection love to get in the way when they can.
## Sickness
When a character passes through infected areas or is subjected directly to filthy environments and conditions they may contract an illness. They may also develop an illness as a cause of natural living - whether from allergies and common colds or unknown sources.

Whenever a character experiences situations that are likely to make them sick, they must make a Sickness Check. Sickness Checks are always made immediately after the next Morning Recovery.

- On a Miss they contract an appropriate illness or one they already have worsens.
- On a Hit they do not get sick, or they progress removing an already existing sickness.

Your character sheet has a Sickness Track that allows for 3 stages of sickness - Minor, Moderate, and Major.

When a character first contracts an illness they mark the Stage 1 of their Sickness Track on their character sheet and mark Tired. Each time the illness worsens they move the track up to the next stage and mark Tired again.

While your Sickness Track is at Stage 2 you suffer a 1 point Hindrance on all Attribute Checks, whether passive or not. This penalty also applies against Medicine Checks made to aid you.

While your Sickness Track is at Stage 3 your Daily Recovery Check has modified results as your body is required to dedicate its efforts more fully towards defeating the illness. Additionally, if a Stage 3 Sickness would worsen, instead lose a Health Level.

When you make your Daily Recovery Check, the first time you would recover a lost Health Level instead move your Sickness Track back a stage once. After Daily Recovery, still make your Sickness Check like normal.

[[Medical Aid]] and [[Health Track#Starred Conditions|starred conditions]] may assist with Sickness Checks just as they do for Daily Recovery.
## Stabilizing & Death
If you lose all 5 Health Levels you are Incapacitated and Dying. 

At the end of the scene you will make a Death Check - the base difficulty is 13, and you’ll roll the greater between Spirit or Vitality.

- On a Fumble, lose your 6th health level. 
- On a Miss, you’re still unstable. The next time you would roll for Daily Recovery instead make a new Death Check. 
- On a Hit, you stabilize. You're still incapacitated & don’t regain any lost Health Levels yet, but this trial won’t be the one to lay you low. 
- On a Triumph, benefit from the effects of a Hit plus you gain Advantage on your next Daily Recovery Check.

If you lose a 6th health level, you die. Your soul will visit the Hall of Journeys, you will have clarity about your life and potential, and there you may return home as you pass through the doors of eternity. The harsh conditions that have affected the world and the struggles of survival have also altered magic itself. Since the displacement of the gods, revival magic has fled from the world.

If you did not die but also did not stabilize, [[Medical Aid]] and [[Health Track#Starred Conditions|starred conditions]] can help with future Death Checks like normal.
