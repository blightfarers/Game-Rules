You have a Health Track which has 5 Health Levels. When something is impactful enough or has built up enough it leads to you losing one of the Levels in your Health Track. If you’ve only got two Health Levels or less remaining you are Battered.

Battered: suffer Disadvantage on all actions that are not about defending, disengaging, suing for peace, or otherwise focused defensively on keeping you alive. If you would become Battered while already so, become Vulnerable instead until one of the effects causing you to be Battered ends.

You will typically lose a Health Level from the cascading effects of Ailing Conditions or from taking damage.

## Taking Damage - Wound Threshold
When you successfully hit with an attack, you may deal damage by rolling your weapon damage and adding the appropriate Attribute - usually Power or Agility, or Potency for spells.

When you take damage, track it on your character sheet beside your Wound Threshold (WT). 

Your WT equals 2 + Vitality + (½ Spirit).

Whenever accumulated damage equals or exceeds your WT, lose one Health Level then subtract WT from the damage total that your tracking. If sufficient damage remains you may have to repeat this process multiple times.

For example, if you have a WT of 3 and take 5 damage, you’ll lose a health level and tally the other 2 points of damage. If you previously had taken 1 damage, instead you’ll end up losing 2 Health Levels with no tally’s for damage remaining.

## Ailing Conditions - Struggling for The Minimums
When it comes to your Ailing Conditions, you’ve got 3 to stay aware of - Tired, Hungry, & Distressed. These are binary states; either you are Hungry, or you're not. While you have an Ailing Condition, put a mark in its checkbox. When you get to remove that condition, erase that mark.

Additionally, if you would have to mark an Ailing Condition while you already have it marked, the effect Cascades, requiring you to mark one of the other two Ailing Conditions. You choose which. If you cannot because all of them are marked, you lose a Health Level instead.

None of the Ailing Conditions apply any penalties while you have them. They are a warning and a buffer - a signal that you are functionally nearing degraded health.

- You mark Tired if you didn’t get at least poor sleep (~ more than 4 hours) or you Fumble a check for Endurance. 
- You mark Hungry if you either didn’t hydrate or get at least 2 poor meals (rations) during the day.
- You mark Distressed when you fail a Check for morale such as due to violence, horror, despair, or deep disgust.

Sometimes your GM will make note of special circumstances that help you flourish and ask you to put a star beside one of the Ailing Conditions. While you have an Ailing Condition starred, the next time you would mark it, remove the star instead.

Each starred condition grants a +1 Asset to your Daily Recovery Check and to healing checks made by your caretakers.

- If you had plenty of sleep (~9+ hours) in comfortable conditions, or took a day off from adventure in a place of safety, you should star Tired.
- If you had a filling and hearty meal or ate well during feasts & festivities, you should star Hungry.
- If you are exceedingly prepared for the path ahead of you, are especially faithfully centered & determined, or uplifted by the true companionship of your fellows, you should star Distressed.

If you have an ailing condition marked but would get to star it, instead remove the mark. This is the primary way to clear marked ailing conditions.
