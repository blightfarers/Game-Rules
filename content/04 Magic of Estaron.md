title: Magic of Estaron
---
Despite the implications of the fragmentation of divine magic, magic at large is still an important part of the world of Estaron. People eventually find ways to adapt, survive, and even thrive. At least until something more comes around to put them down again.

Throughout history there have been many magical theories and traditions but at the root of it all a mage must be able to attune to the forces of magic, channel it by their mind & will, and withstand the consequences that come to mortals from wielding such powers themselves.

At the heart of all magic lies Mana. Mana is the ethereal energy that flows through and around all things. Throughout history countless philosophies have attempted to explain its nature and how it may best be wielded. Some manipulate it through instinct, others through careful study, experimentation, and practice, but every mage ultimately draws upon the same supernatural force.

The way Mana is accessed varies between traditions. Some mages carry it within themselves, while others prepare vessels, techniques, or rituals that allow them to safely channel the same force. 

A mage's Mana Pool represents the amount of Mana they can safely draw upon, contain, and direct before their body and spirit begin suffering the consequences. When your Mana Pool is at 0, you may continue casting spells, but doing so forces you to bear the cost yourself.

Your magical tradition outlines how your Mana Pool works.

## Casting Spells
Each spell is a specific magical effect with its own Mana cost, associated magical domains, and unique effects. To cast a spell, spend Mana equal to its Mana cost, then make a Spellcasting Check as you feed the shape the creative forces of the world into being.

On a Hit, the spell succeeds and resolves its effects as described. On a Miss the Mana is spent but the spell does not take effect.

If you cannot pay a spell's full Mana Cost, spend as much Mana as you have remaining. Then suffer Mana Feedback once for each unpaid Mana.

Your magical tradition prescribes which Attribute you use to cast your spells.

## Empowering Spells
Most spells have optional upgrades listed with it called Empowerments - effectively making each spell its own modifiable toolkit. 

As you cast a spell you can choose to upgrade it by paying the additional mana cost of one or more Empowerments and adding their Hindrance into your Spellcasting Check.

If your spell succeeds, the spell resolves normally but with additional or modified effects as described in the Empowerment. If an Empowered spell fails, suffer Mana Feedback once.

## Mana Limit
Power is not only limited by how much Mana you possess, but by how much you can safely channel at once.

	Your Mana Limit is equal to {Spellcasting Attribute + Spirit}.

At the end of each of your turns, total all Mana you spent during that turn. If the amount spent exceeds your Mana Limit, suffer Mana Feedback once for each point above your Mana Limit.

If you cannot pay a spell's full Mana Cost and you exceed your Mana Limit the effects stack - meaning you can end up doubling up on Mana Feedback.

## Mana Feedback
Whenever you suffer Mana Feedback, roll 1d6 with no modifiers and apply the result.

| Mana Feedback Roll | Result.<br>                                                                                                                   |
| -----------------: | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
|              **6** | Re-roll. If you roll **3**, **4**, or **5**, double the effects, but only once regardless of how many re-rolls are performed. |
|              **5** | Suffer the effects of rolling both **3** and **4**.                                                                           |
|              **4** | Take **3** points of unblockable damage.                                                                                      |
|              **3** | Mark the **Tired** Ailing Survival Condition.                                                                                 |
|              **2** | Nothing.                                                                                                                      |
|              **1** | Nothing.                                                                                                                      |

If you would suffer Mana Feedback multiple times at once, simply make this roll multiple times, resolving each separately but immediately.

## Magical Traditions

### Spell Blood
Your magic is not based on training, practice, or study. Magic is part of your blood. It's instinct and intention driven by your will -and the world itself responds. The Attribute you use for Spellcasting Checks is Spirit.

You have a Mana Pool equal to {Vitality + Potency + Spirit * 2}. You regain 1d6 Mana +1 for each Starred Ailing Condition every time you roll Daily Recovery.

You know a number of spells at character creation equal to your Intuition.

### Awakened
Your magic is born from deep attunement to the infinite possibilities found within your mind, soul, and the world around you. Whether through qi, psionics, or supernatural harmony with the primordial forces of nature, you shape reality by entering states of profound resonance rather than through study or instinct alone.

Your Mana Pool represents your Resonance—the harmony you have cultivated with these greater forces. You recover Mana through methods unique to your tradition.

Awakened mages possess Mantles: distinct states of body, mind, and spirit that bring them into accord with particular supernatural principles. While a Mantle is active, you gain its passive benefit and may cast its associated spells by spending Mana. You may have only one Mantle active at a time.

Your Spellcasting Attribute is Intuition.

### Spell Wright
Your magic is diligently earned through consistent practice, study, experimentation, and refinement. You draw on the ambient energies of the world around you to summon up Mana for your spells and utilize contraptions and enchantments to directly support your spell casting. The Attribute you use for Spellcasting Checks is Reason.

Your Mana Pool is 0, and never gains Mana on its own. Instead you Gather Mana as an action.

Gather Mana: roll 2d10 + Reason. Divide the total by 4, ignoring any remainder. Add the result to your Mana Pool. Gathering Mana requires a full turn or is done freely as part of activating a Mana Anchor.

#### Mana Overflow
You can safely hold only so much Mana. If your Mana Pool ever exceeds twice your Mana Limit, immediately interrupt the current action. Your Mana Anchor collapses, all Mana in your Mana Pool is lost, and any spell you were casting fails before taking effect. This is called Mana Overflow.

#### Spell Harness
Additionally, Spell Wrights have developed a unique ability to construct a Spell Harness to help protect themselves and enhance the spells they specialize in most.

A Spell Harness is a magical framework established to make casting powerful spells more stable. There are many different Spell Harnesses, and each is learned separately from actual spells. 

Each Spell Harness is also tied to one or more magical domains - keywords that signify what supernatural forces it is built for such as Shadow, Earth, or Gravity. The benefits of your Spell Harness only applies to spells you cast that share at least one domain with the Harness. For example, a Water domain Harness does not give its benefits to Fire domain spells.

Generating a Spell Harness has a Mana Cost and is a single action, but its benefits don’t apply until the end of the turn where you initiated it. If you cannot pay its full Mana cost, suffer Mana Feedback for each unpaid Mana to activate it,otherwise the Harness collapses and the action is wasted.

When you create a Spell Harness, make a Spellcasting Check with a difficulty of 13. On a Miss the Harness creation fails. On a Hit it forms and provides a +1 Asset. On a Triumph it instead provides a +2 Asset, and you regain Mana equal to its Mana Cost.

While your Mana Harness remains active:

- You gain the Assets granted when you created the Harness on Spellcasting Checks for spells that share its domain.
- So long as your Empowerment Hindrances are less than or equal to your Harness Assets, do not suffer Mana Feedback from an Empowered Spell failing.
- Spells with a matching domain cost 1 less Mana to cast.

You may have only one Harness active at a time. You may dismiss it freely. Creating a new Spell Harness replaces your current one.

A Spell Harness may remain active through ordinary activity. Whenever a significant passage of time occurs, such as travel, exploration, or rest, maintaining it requires paying its Mana cost again. If you cannot pay this cost, you may continue maintaining the Harness by suffering Mana Feedback once for each Mana you could not pay.

Your Mana Harness draws the essence of its domain into the world around you, causing the magic you wield to become visible and tangible. Embers may drift through the air, the earth may seem unnaturally steady beneath your feet, or nearby shadows may deepen and stir.

Where violence, suspicion, or fear are concerns, maintaining a Spell Harness is much like openly carrying a loaded gun. Others can plainly see that you are prepared to wield powerful magic, and that should make them wary.

Example Mana Harnesses
Flame Harness (1 Mana): Fire Domain.
Earth Harness (1 Mana): Earth Domain.
Shadow Harness (1 Mana): Shadow Domain.
Storm Harness (2 Mana): Water, Wind, and Shock Domains.
Dragon Harness (2 Mana): Fire Domain but also gain +3 Fire Protection
