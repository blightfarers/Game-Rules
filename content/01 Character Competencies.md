## Attributes
Your character competencies begin with your Attributes, of which you have 8. They are: <u>_Power, Agility, Vitality, Perception, Reason, Intuition, Presence, and Spirit_</u>. Each stands for something broadly, and we expect each to come into play depending on the circumstances and needs of the moment as well as the approach, style, and methods you use as you take action.

- **Power** represents strength, force, and explosiveness. 
- **Agility** is about coordination, precision, and deftness. 
- **Vitality** includes health, endurance and constitution. 
- **Potency** affects the efficacy and power of spells.
- **Reason** suggests logic, procedure, & knowledge. Tied to strategic searching.
- **Intuition** implies instinct, tacit understanding, and creativity. Tied to passive perception.
- **Presence** is tied to social atmosphere, projecting intention or hiding it, entertaining, & leading.
- **Spirit** is largely about your focus, faith, willpower, and mettle.

These Attributes set the tone for ***how*** you are most likely to succeed - both where you are skilled and where you are outside of your comfort zone. They also affect some other numbers on your character sheet. Your other competencies start to fill in ***what*** you are actually good at in more detail.

Often, depending on how you address a problem, multiple different Attributes could be used. The Attribute that most directly applies in comparison to the other Attributes is what you use when you roll the dice.

Each Attribute will have a Rank between 0 and 5. Anything 2 or better is at least noteworthy. Anything 4 and above is exceptional. 

Each time the Game Facilitator has your [[02 Making A Check]] you'll add one of these Attributes to the roll.
## Background
Everyone has an origin - something that is part of their personal history before the start of the game. This is called your character Background. 

In short, everyone had a homeland, community, & skillset.

Your Background includes at least two details, one that describes who you were or what you were doing, and another that describes the circumstances surrounding you during that era. It would typically be written as "{Profession or Archetype} of {Location, Community, Event}” or otherwise includes the same details.

Examples include:

- Refugee of the Hylandian Wars
- Pirate of the Azure Seas
- Horizon Chasers Guild, Wilderness Guide
- Arkel-Leuom Street Sweeper
- Demon Hunter from The Shattered Isles
- Blackwater League Merchant

You can actually have multiple Backgrounds. Each will have a Rank associated with it. Its Rank gets added to any rolls where the skills, knowledge, attention to detail, or sociality seem applicable. If more than one Background applies, each adds their Rank to the roll. 

Your Backgrounds can even have shared experience. If you have 2 Ranks in being a Wilderness Guide for the traveling merchants in Hylandia, that doesn’t preclude you from being a Wilderness Guide for someone or somewhere else, or from also having been a caravan guard. A guide might not have done much fighting, but a guard would have. You could have been both.

Without a feature counting your character as Trained they are, by default, Untrained. This means you’ll suffer a 2 point Hindrance on the roll for common things like swimming, fighting, or negotiating a sale. For especially complex, intricate, or unique skills (like picking locks, an advanced martial maneuver, or casting a spell without training) you’ll instead take Disadvantage. 

Whenever a Background applies, you're automatically Trained, even if it’s only Rank 1. An individual Background may not have more than 3 Ranks. You must have at least one Background with at least 1 Rank in it.
## Talents
You can also learn and specialize in specific skills rather than simply develop broad proficiency. As you develop individual skills specifically you can gain Ranks in a Talent. If a Talent applies you are Trained and it adds its Rank to your Check.

A Talent may not have more than 3 Ranks.

That's the basics of character competencies. But, how competent you are hardly matters if you can’t [[03 Character Health|survive]].
