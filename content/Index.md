# Setting Introduction
>*"… Estaron is a nasty place now. Lore tells of a time that it was a place of paradise, but these days what once was life, magic, and legend has been drained. Life sucked out of nature itself. The earth is dried, blackened, and cracked. The trees have faded and darkened. Animals are few and far between, assuming they aren’t rotters too, and the sky is shadowed despite the sun."*
>
>*Large swaths of Estaron are like this - zones where the world itself is wounded. The dead walk. They ravenously hunt those things that still have life and will. Yet even after vile consumption they still find their hunger dissatisfied. These are the blighted lands.*
>
>*Why? You ask? The exact truth is lost. Exactly how it happened, no one knows. Who has the time to find out anyways? It is known only that in his war against humanity Mazut rose - a lich unlike any other. He made them bow to him, the undying lords, and brought the forces of their hordes together as they started systematically tearing the magic of our ancient homeland apart.*
>
>*That is, before he cut us off from divinity, and ascended to take their place. It is said that when you die, it is he who offers you a twisted second chance. But such things are a curse. You think he would freely help mere playthings like us? After his ascension he abandoned his war - leaving behind the armies he had made, I suppose finding a greater method to exact his hatred. Many of those abominations still wander, aimlessly, hungrily. **
>
>*Damn him. Stabbing at life and hope itself but leaving before he finished the job. The undying lords hunted us, used us, even farmed us. Pockets of Estaron remain unblighted, just enough to have us squabbling amongst ourselves for the bare scraps of survival. Enough to keep us around without giving us hope of resistance. **
>
>*And if such a force does begin to rise, it’s quashed, horrifyingly stomped out before being used to re-stock what was lost.*
>
>*Even worse? We’re trapped. Fort Sill guards the only southern pass into Hylandia. The acrid sea, now the source of the ocean's undead haunts thoughts of fleeing along the east, sharp cliffs useful only for an early death border the storm tossed seas between us and Dral’Kannum - a place only better because at least it’s monsters can die - and if you dare travel northwest you’ll find the heart of the blight - the hollowed mountain. **
>
>*Only those with more than a death wish journey there.*
>
>*At least if you were exiled here you’ll remember a better place. But if you had the bad luck to be born here, all you’ll ever know is blood and death - or worse… “*

Again and again civilization has fallen to the temptations of darkness and brought upon themselves their own curse. From the shattered lands of Drenor where oblivion crawls forth from dark portals to Dral’Kannum where greed and desperation brought about the treaty that plunders the world's most primal lands, Hylandia where those few nations that remain were brought to their knees through wartorn infighting, or Estaron - the first land, a paradise named after the world itself - now blighted from sea to sea with undeath by Mazut before ascending as the lich-god, leaving only pockets of society remaining.

You are a sojourner in those blighted lands, surrounded by danger on every turn, the hunger of both the living and the damned, forced to spend each day toiling for the scraps of survival. Yet, with perhaps a justifiable defense for despair, you continue anyways. Years before all of mortality was separated from divinity, and Estaron walled off from the rest of the world. Yet you remain.

Who are you? Why do you yet endure? Who will you become?

This is the first installment of a line of roleplaying games where players survive terrible conditions and play through what changes they can wreak in the world. The lore of this world is deep and explorable. It starts beyond the stars, is built around them, and eventually beyond those limitless bounds it will return. The danger of this world is real - but despite those conditions, so is its hope. Discover how and why for yourself as you play Blight Farers: Liches Killed the Gods™.

At the start of the game you will hop into a tutorial session that teaches you the basics of play. During this tutorial, or at least before the next session, you fill out your character sheet and finalize details about who they are and where they are most competent. As you make your character, you need to understand what you will be facing.

Blight Farers is a TTRPG that focuses on creative thinking, working against constraints, and navigating within dilemmas. Everything has its impact. Your character can potentially be anyone and become anything. That is, if they can make it through the day.

Because of the dynamics which you will both suffer and enjoy in this game, think of it like a game of heroic survival. Everything is against you. You’ll have to manage yourself well. And you’ll need to take care of your team. Inconsiderate care and betrayal often lead to significant loss. But, simultaneously to all that, you're not a victim - you're an agent in a dark world that’s trying to take advantage of you. 

There will always be something you can do, a shred of hope to draw on against all odds, and even something to enjoy in the darkest of times. In fact, the gloom you face on your adventures is secretly a miracle, and one day that deeper light will rise from obscurity. Your darkest games will be a fond memory. And maybe even some personal practice.

If that is what your gaming table experiences, you're playing Blight Farers well.

As you make decisions about your character, or as they make choices under pressure with competing goals, who they are in the world and in the story becomes more real. You will not spend hours up front dedicated solely to a starting character - but as they continue, they will also grow.

In order to support the constraints of a survival TTRPG alongside the flexibility needed for stories of despair and transformation the game engine implements principle based structure - aimed to provide constraints and drive behavior while simultaneously leaving the door open for ingenuity, creativity, and GM fiat.

With all of that in mind, this game will ask both players and GMs to think a bit differently from traditional TTRPGs. This framework starts with Character Competencies




From competencies to health & magic, these core concepts and patterns set the frame for the game. You’ll still have equipment, lineage traits, and more than fills out your character and handles the nuances of the game - but from here you’ll understand how the dynamics of the system work together to invite play in a difficult world.



Creator’s Postword
Behind the scenes this game aims to explore several questions and principles - things I am personally studying out & learning to put my feelings into words. These are:

No matter what, there is always something you can say, do, learn, or practice. 
If you don’t consciously & actively choose what you focus on, something else will.
How can something horrible, terrifying, or even catastrophic be part of something wonderful?
How can I tell the difference between something that seems good but is actually a distraction or a sinkhole, and something that is truly a worthy influence in the decisions & attitudes of my life?
What is it that makes hope and unfeigned goodness intelligent & strategic - not merely virtuous?

During my childhood at times we would be affected by the winds of hurricanes and other severe storms even though we were rarely in the most directly impacted areas. Our family would go out for walks during these and other lighter storms. These family walks are very positive memories of my earlier years - from watching trees fall and feeling the impact of the crash through the ground to swimming through minor floods and the clean up & community help that came afterwards - I learned to enjoy, explore, play, and uplift in a place where most others would, quite reasonably, rather not step outside.

Later on this became an analogy for me - where many see doom and dread I was taught to experience hope and enthusiasm. I wanted to share that through one of my favorite hobbies - tabletop roleplaying.

While this is first and foremost a game, I love when people take things from games that are useful to real life. I have often felt that wholesome play is one of the best strengtheners for mind, body, spirit, and community. So, I hope that as you play through your characters' challenges - as unfair as the game is designed to make them feel - that you get a little something to help you in your life as well, even if it’s just comfort, rest, and friendship. Or practice.

Your storms may be terrible, but they are a reason to get hopeful and enthusiastic, a sign of something greater to come. And a miracle all on their own. Don’t be so distracted by your storms that you forget to set your sights on what really matters. You are the person who has the brutal opportunity to make the best of your “beneficial” adversaries and to find rest in your storms. That may be an unfair challenge - but there's value and purpose to the principle.

I look forward and upward, to you, Storm Walker.
